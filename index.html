What does a mad scientist do?

A Mad Scientist invents driven by curiosity and exploration to benefit all. With this mixture you never know what's going to pop up!

Okay, so what is this?

Adventure Scientist is a unique self identification that captures my willingness to try new things, push boundaries, and say yes, with my passion for experimentation, observation, and learning.

What's this built with?

This page utilizes a one-page theme and is deployed on Github Pages. I am a self taught computer scientist with years of tinkering with web development.

Inspired by nature's feats of engineering, I never fail to find parallels to my own professional work.

Intelligent Fusion technology, Inc (IFT) is a small, high-tech, R&D and engineering company that produces products across the industry sectors.

Graduate Work

General (2009-2014)

  • Deigned and built heaters, sample holders, thermocouples, shutter mechanisms, vacuum chamber port designation, and polished drilled, tapped, and metal coated mirrors for FTIR optics.
  • Developed Standard Operating Procedures for two home built FITRs, thermal evaporator, ion-beam evaporator, e-beam evaporator, and Zeta Potential.

Study of the relationship between osteoblast adhesion, physical topography, and surface chemistry (2012)

Description: Investigated the small company, Titan Spine's proprietary surface technology and how it actively promotes the bone fusion process.
Characterized the near surface elemental gradient, the surface elemental composition, and investigated the surface energy of a chemically and mechanically roughened titanium alloys. Learned how to act quickly to fulfill time sensitive demands of the biological coordinators and the FDA. Discovered the importance of proprietary information to a company and the limits it puts on discussions with outside parties.
Lessons Learned: Proprietary interactions with business. Relationship between surface structures and surface chemical gradients.
Experimental Techniques: ion-milled SEM, XPS, AES, Optical Profilometry, Zeta-Potential, contact angle, UV-ozone cleaning, plasma etch cleaning
Computational Tools: bash scripts

Viscoelastic Properties of Protein Films on micro-QCM Arrays (2012)

Description: Characterized the thickness, density, elastic modulus, and viscosity of five different globular blood proteins adsorbed on hydrophobic, hydrophilic and charged surfaces via micromachined quartz crystal resonator arrays.
Lessons Learned: QCM theory. Thiol based SAM. Protein chemistry and surface adhesion. RAIRS techniques and SAM themodynamics.
Experimental Techniques: SERS Raman, AFM, thermal thin film deposition
Computational Tools: MATLAB, FORTRAN

Cyanide Interaction with Gold Surfaces (2011)

Description: Experimentally and computationally resolved the bonding coordination of cyanide on gold surfaces and computationally discovered the adsorbed cyanide electronic structure. When cyanide gas was exposed to gold thin films CN self-assembled monolayers were formed.
The Quantum Espresso program was self-taught to determine the bond energy and vibrational frequency of cyanide and isocyanide headgroup bonding on gold and palladium surfaces and metal clusters. The systems were compared to establish the strengths and weaknesses of the plane-wave method applied to isolated molecules in vacuum.
Lessons Learned: How to use and submit jobs to HPC clusters. Quality thin film deposition under ultra high vacuum. RAIRS techniques and SAM thermodynamics.
Experimental Techniques: RAIRS FTIR, AFM, contact angle, thin film depositions, HRXPS, SERS Raman, Waveguide Raman
Computational Tools: DFT, Quantum Espresso
